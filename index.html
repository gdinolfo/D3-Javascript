Obesity and Poverty Rate Coorelation

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The visualization above was meant to determine if there is a positive correlation between poverty and obesity. There is a visible trend that suggests that the poorer states tend to have higher incidences of obesity. Arkansas ranks the highest in terms of obesity and poverty, while states such as Colorado and Hawaii enjoy economic prosperity and a fitter population. However, the trend is not definitive, there are plenty of states with low poverty and high obesity such as New Hampshire and Maryland.
